# Delve — Product Vision

## The Problem

People spend hours daily scrolling through content that leaves them feeling
empty. They *want* to learn — but the gap between "I'm curious about nuclear
energy" and actually understanding it is filled with 45-minute YouTube videos,
impenetrable Wikipedia articles, and ChatGPT conversations that vanish when you
close the tab.

Learning should feel as effortless and addictive as scrolling — but leave you
smarter.

## The Solution

Delve replaces mindless scrolling with AI-powered knowledge exploration. Pick
any topic, get an instant interactive breakdown, and drill deeper into whatever
catches your eye. Every exploration builds a persistent, growing knowledge base
that's uniquely yours.

## Core Value Proposition

**Learn anything, one tap at a time.**

Delve turns curiosity into understanding through a simple loop:

1. **Pick a topic** — type anything or choose an AI suggestion
2. **See the big picture** — get a hierarchical breakdown in seconds
3. **Go deeper** — tap any section to expand, add subtopics, or branch out
4. **Build knowledge** — your explorations persist and connect over time
5. **Test yourself** — quiz mode validates understanding

## What Makes Delve 10x Better

| Alternative | Friction | Delve Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| Wikipedia | Overwhelming, linear, no personalization | Hierarchical, bite-sized, grows with you |
| YouTube | Time-consuming, passive, hard to reference | Interactive, scannable, persistent |
| ChatGPT | Conversations are linear and ephemeral | Structured, visual, saved forever |
| Textbooks | Expensive, rigid, one-size-fits-all | Free-form, adaptive, curiosity-driven |
| Podcasts | Linear, no way to drill deeper | Non-linear, interactive, explorable |

## The "Aha!" Moment

A user types "Surfing" and within 3 seconds has a beautiful, collapsible
knowledge tree covering wave physics, technique, culture, safety, and gear —
each with rich, sourced content. They tap "How Swells Form," hit Expand, and
get a deep-dive with real data and citations. They add a subtopic on "Surf
Forecasting" and suddenly they're learning about buoy networks and swell
models. 10 minutes later they've built a personal knowledge map they can
return to anytime.

That's the moment they stop opening Instagram and open Delve instead.

## Target User

**The Curious Scroller** — someone who:
- Has broad intellectual curiosity but limited time
- Currently fills idle moments with social media
- Wants to feel like they're growing, not just consuming
- Values depth over breadth but needs an easy entry point
- Uses their phone as their primary device

## Design Principles

1. **Curiosity-first** — Every screen should spark "what's in there?"
2. **Zero friction** — From idea to knowledge in under 5 seconds
3. **Depth on demand** — Start simple, go as deep as you want
4. **Your knowledge, your way** — No prescribed learning paths
5. **Grounded in reality** — Every fact sourced, every claim verifiable
